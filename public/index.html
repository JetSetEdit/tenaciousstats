What the numbers and labels on this dashboard mean. On the main screen, hover a label for a short explanation.

Main numbers (overview)

Word Meaning
Visits (total) How many times people opened the site in this period. One person can count more than once if they come back later.
People (approx.) Rough count of different people who visited in this period.
Page Views Total times any page on the site was loaded or viewed, including repeats.
How involved visitors were Share of visits where people did something meaningful — several pages, a longer stay, or an important action.
Typical visit length Average time people actively spent on the site during one visit.
Quick exits Share of visits where someone left after a single page without much interaction.

Where visits came from

Word Meaning

(direct) / (none) Visits with no clear referrer – usually people typing the URL, using bookmarks, or coming from untagged links.

Word Meaning

New vs returning

Word Meaning
New visitors First-time visits on this device or browser.

Understanding Growth & Percentage Change

Why do some changes exceed 100%?

A percentage change above 100% simply means the metric has more than doubled. For example:

  • 100% Increase: The value doubled (e.g., from 10 to 20).
  • 200% Increase: The value tripled (e.g., from 10 to 30).
  • 900% Increase: The value grew 10 times larger (e.g., from 2 to 20).

Infinity or >999%: This occurs when the baseline (previous period) was zero or very close to it. If you had 0 visits last month and 10 this month, the growth is mathematically "infinite," which we display as >999% to keep the report clean.

Inverted Metrics: For metrics like Bounce Rate, a decrease (▼) is considered a "Good" trend, while for most others, an increase (▲) is positive.
